# Research Framing

This project studies reliability-aware machine learning for noisy health signals. ECG is the initial domain because the MIT-BIH dataset provides a practical benchmark for physiological signal inference. The broader direction connects to signal processing, speech science, and machine learning for health assessment, including research directions associated with Visar Berisha at Arizona State University.

Health signals are noisy observational measurements. A model should therefore be evaluated not only by accuracy, but also by calibration, robustness, uncertainty, and stability under degraded acquisition conditions.

## Supervisor and Long-Term Public-Health Alignment

In this project, Visar Berisha is framed as a prospective supervisor or research mentor alignment because his work connects signal processing, speech and hearing science, machine learning, and health assessment. The ECG implementation is an initial reliability-aware physiological signal testbed, while the long-term direction extends toward voice, cough, respiratory audio, speech, and language signals.

This creates a coherent bridge to Africa CDC-style public-health goals: reliable disease surveillance from noisy, incomplete, low-cost observational signals. In low-resource healthcare environments, signals collected through smartphones or community devices could support future surveillance research for respiratory infections, tuberculosis, and emerging outbreaks, provided the systems are rigorously validated and responsibly governed.
