# Audio Surveillance Governance Checklist

Future cough, speech, and respiratory-audio surveillance research requires additional governance beyond ECG benchmark experiments.

## Privacy and Consent

- [ ] Obtain informed consent for audio collection.
- [ ] Explain whether speech content or only acoustic features are used.
- [ ] Minimize storage of identifiable raw audio.
- [ ] Define retention and deletion policies.
- [ ] Protect metadata such as location, language, and health status.

## Fairness and Representation

- [ ] Evaluate across languages and dialects.
- [ ] Evaluate across device types and recording environments.
- [ ] Report performance across regions and demographic groups where available.
- [ ] Avoid deployment claims from narrow benchmark data.

## Public-Health Use

- [ ] Clarify whether the system is for research, screening support, or surveillance signal detection.
- [ ] Require human/public-health expert review before action.
- [ ] Report uncertainty and calibration.
- [ ] Monitor distribution shift over time.

## Africa CDC Alignment

The most responsible framing is decision support for surveillance research, not autonomous diagnosis. The system should help identify signals worth investigation, not replace clinical testing or public-health judgment.
