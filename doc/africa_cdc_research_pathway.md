# Long-Term Research Pathway: Reliable Health-Signal ML for Africa-Focused Disease Surveillance

## Vision

The long-term goal of this research direction is to develop reliable machine learning methods for extracting actionable health information from noisy observational signals, with applications ranging from physiological monitoring to public-health surveillance systems in Africa.

A future Africa CDC-oriented project could study voice-based and audio-based disease surveillance using low-cost signal collection from smartphones or community health devices. Candidate signals include:

- cough recordings;
- speech recordings;
- respiratory sounds;
- other low-cost physiological or acoustic signals.

Potential surveillance targets include:

- tuberculosis;
- respiratory infections;
- outbreak signals from emerging infectious diseases;
- population-level changes in respiratory health patterns.

## Why This Connects to Visar Berisha's Research Direction

This project treats Professor Visar Berisha's research area as a potential training ground for the methodological core of the long-term goal: reliable inference from noisy, incomplete, real-world health signals.

The connection is not that this repository directly implements a Berisha lab speech model. The connection is deeper and methodological:

- signal processing for health assessment;
- machine learning on noisy observational signals;
- speech, voice, language, and physiological biomarkers;
- robust inference under acquisition variability;
- trustworthy confidence estimates for health-related decisions.

ECG is the current testbed because it is a structured physiological signal with public benchmark data. Voice, cough, respiratory audio, and speech-language data are natural next modalities.

## Why This Connects to Africa CDC's Mission

Many African health systems face constraints that make reliable low-cost signal inference important:

- limited specialist availability;
- limited diagnostic equipment;
- remote and mobile populations;
- delayed surveillance signals;
- noisy, incomplete, and heterogeneous observational data.

Voice and respiratory audio can potentially be collected with smartphones or low-cost devices. This makes signal-based disease surveillance attractive, but only if the models are reliable, calibrated, robust to noise, and validated responsibly.

## Responsible Framing

This pathway should be framed as a research ambition, not a deployed clinical or surveillance claim. Any future disease surveillance system would require external validation, ethical review, clinical/public-health collaboration, privacy protection, subgroup evaluation, and deployment studies in the target context.
